Operating finance brief

GTM Finance Workflow

From commercial activity to unit economics and recurring decisions.

GTM finance work has to connect what the commercial team is doing with when revenue starts, how margin changes, and what the forecast should assume.

Pipeline, signed deals, partner activity, and campaign volume are not economic value by themselves. They become useful finance signals only when they convert into revenue, activate at a measurable pace, ramp, repeat, and contribute margin after GTM cost and cost-to-serve.

The goal is not more reporting. The goal is to know what changed, whether the change is real, and whether it should change the forecast or the GTM decision.

Three checks I would run

For each area, I would show the operating flow, the data check, and the decision signal.

Lane
Operating flow
Validation check
Decision signal
1

Commercial flow

Is activity moving toward revenue quickly enough?

Pipeline→ Qualified→ Signed→ First revenue→ 90-day ramp
Check: match signed date, first revenue date, and 30/60/90-day ramp using consistent account / partner / campaign IDs.
Decision signal: signed activity should not be treated as near-term revenue until activation timing and early ramp are visible.
2

Economic conversion

Does activated revenue produce margin and payback?

Activated revenue→ Direct cost→ Support load→ Margin→ Payback
Check: reconcile activated revenue to direct cost, support load, GTM cost allocation, contribution margin, and payback.
Decision signal: revenue is useful only if margin and payback support more GTM spend.
3

Revenue quality

Is revenue repeatable, stable, and forecastable?

First revenue cohort→ Repeat activity→ Expansion / drop-off→ Margin trend→ Forecast variance
Check: track cohorts after first revenue for repeat behavior, expansion/drop-off, margin trend, and actual-versus-forecast variance.
Decision signal: first revenue has to repeat, hold margin, and explain forecast misses.
I would define the finance checks that a SQL or BigQuery workflow needs to support. I would not call this production data architecture unless I owned that build directly.

Appendix: sample validation outputs

Check Expected output What it tests
Activation timing Signed count; % activated; median days to first revenue; 30/60/90-day ramp. Whether signed activity is turning into revenue fast enough for the forecast.
Margin / payback Activated revenue; direct cost %; support cost %; contribution margin %; estimated payback. Whether revenue clears cost-to-serve and supports more GTM spend.
Durability / variance M1/M3/M6 revenue; repeat or expansion %; margin trend; actual vs forecast variance. Whether first revenue repeats, holds margin, and explains forecast movement.

Relevant operating-finance proof

This is close to work I have done directly at SMS. The work was not just reporting results. It meant connecting pricing, utilization, account profitability, billing discipline, margin, cash, and headcount timing to the decisions the CEO and COO needed to make.

The business model here is different, so I would not claim one-to-one experience. The transferable part is the work: learn how the business creates revenue, identify where activity turns into economic value, test whether that revenue produces margin, and give leaders a review they can actually use.

Recent project work
